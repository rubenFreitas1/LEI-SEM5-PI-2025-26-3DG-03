# Glossary

| **_TEA_** (EN)                          | **_Description_** (EN)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :-------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Administrator**                       | responsible for managing user accounts and permissions, ensuring that each profile has access only to the functions appropriate to their role.                                                                                                                              |
| **Berth**                               | is a specific location alongside a dock where a vessel is secured, allowing safe access for cargo handling and port services.                                                                                                                                               |
| **Cargo**                               | refers to the goods or containers carried by a vessel for loading or unloading at a port.                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Cargo Manifest**                      | is a structured document submitted by the shipping agent that lists all containers for a vessel, including their identification, contents, and positions on the vessel.                                                                                                     |
| **Cargo Type**                          | category or nature of goods being transported, such as refrigerated items, electronics, hazardous materials, general consumer products, or oversized industrial equipment.                                                                                                  |
| **Container**                           | are standardized, reusable cargo units used to transport goods. They come in various sizes and types (e.g., refrigerated, hazardous, oversized).                                                                                                                            |
| **Container Yards**                     | are designated areas within a port where shipping containers are temporarily stored before loading onto vessels or after unloading.                                                                                                                                         |
| **Crane**                               | mechanical lifting equipment used to move containers and cargo between vessels, storage areas, and trucks. Types include fixed STS cranes at docks and mobile yard cranes in container yards.                                                                               |
| **Crew Member**                         | An individual working on a vessel, including the captain and other staff, responsible for operating the ship safely and complying with port regulations.                                                                                                                    |
| **Dock**                                | also called a quay, is a designated area where vessels are moored to perform loading, unloading, and other port operations.                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Expected Arrival**                    | represents the expected date and time when a vessel is planned to arrive at the port.                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Expected Departure**                  | represents the projected date and time when a vessel is expected to leave the port after completing its operations.                                                                                                                                                         |
| **ETA**                                 | abbreviation for Expected Arrival.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **ETD**                                 | abbreviation for Expected Departure.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Facilities**                          | physical infrastructure of the port, including docks, container yards, and warehouses, used to support the handling, storage, and movement of vessels and cargo.                                                                                                            |
| **Feeder Vessels**                      | small ships serving regional routes and connecting to larger ports.                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Human Resources**                     | port personnel, such as crane operators, truck drivers, yard planners, and other qualified staff, whose availability and skills are critical for executing port operations.                                                                                                 |
| **IMO**                                 | abbreviation for International Maritime Organization.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **International Maritime Organization** | a specialized United Nations agency responsible for regulating international shipping. It assigns unique identification numbers to vessels, sets safety, security, and environmental standards, and ensures global maritime compliance.                                     |
| **ISO 6346:2022 Standard**              | is the international standard for container identification. Each container is assigned a unique code.                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Loading Manifest**                    | lists all containers to be loaded onto the vessel for departure.                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Logistics Operator**                  | responsible for defining and scheduling operational tasks for approved Vessel Visits, including unloading, loading, and resource allocation.                                                                                                                                |
| **Mobile Resources**                    | a subset of physical resources that can move around the port to perform tasks, such as yard cranes, trucks, and terminal tractors, as opposed to fixed equipment like dock-installed STS cranes.                                                                            |
| **Operational Window**                  | the scheduled periods when resources or staff are available to perform tasks, e.g., weekly schedules or shifts for cranes, trucks, and operating personnel.                                                                                                                 |
| **Operations**                          | activities involved in handling vessels and cargo at the port, including loading, unloading, transporting, and storage, as well as the allocation of resources to complete these tasks efficiently.                                                                         |
| **Panamax Vessels**                     | the maximum size that fits through the old Panama Canal locks, typically carrying ~5,000 containers.                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Physical Resources**                  | equipment used for port logistics, such as cranes, trucks, and terminal tractors, which enable the movement and handling of cargo.                                                                                                                                          |
| **Port**                                | a complex infrastructure where vessels arrive, unload, store, and load cargo. It includes docks, container yards, warehouses, and other supporting facilities managed by the Port Authority.                                                                                |
| **Port Authority**                      | responsible for managing these facilities, ensuring that docks are allocated appropriately, yard capacity is monitored, and warehouses are operated according to port regulations and safety standards.                                                                     |
| **Port Authority Officer**              | responsible for defining and scheduling operational tasks for approved Vessel Visits, including unloading, loading, and resource allocation.                                                                                                                                |
| **Post Panamax Vessels**                | larger, requiring deeper berths and more cranes.                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Qualification**                       | certification or skill set required by staff to operate specific resources safely, e.g., an STS crane operator must be certified to handle the crane.                                                                                                                       |
| **Shipping Agent Organization**         | are organizations that represent vessel owners or operators in port operations. Their primary responsibility is to coordinate administrative and operational tasks associated with a vessel visit.                                                                          |
| **Shipping Agent Representative**       | responsible for submitting Vessel Visit notifications and associated Cargo Manifests for vessels they are authorized to represent.                                                                                                                                          |
| **Staff**                               | personnel employed to perform port operations, including both human resources involved in moving cargo and supervising tasks.                                                                                                                                               |
| **Ship-To-Store Cranes**                | cranes permanently installed at docks, used to load and unload containers directly between vessels and storage areas.                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Single Page Access**                  | is a web application that loads a single HTML page and dynamically updates its content as the user interacts with it, without requiring full page reloads.                                                                                                                  |
| **SPA**                                 | abbreviation of Single Page Access.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Storage Area**                        | designated locations within the port, such as container yards or warehouses, where cargo is temporarily stored before loading, after unloading, or while awaiting further processing.                                                                                       |
| **STS Cranes**                          | abbreviation for Ship-To-Store Cranes.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Terminal Tractors**                   | vehicles used to transport containers between docks, yards, and warehouses within the port.                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **TEU**                                 | abbreviation for Twenty-Foot Equivalent Unit.                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Twenty-Foot Equivalent Unit**         | standard measurement, assuming all containers have the same dimension.                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Unloading Manifest**                  | lists all containers to be offloaded from the vessel upon arrival.                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **ULCVs**                               | abbreviation for Ultra Large Container Vessels.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Ultra Large Container Vessels**       | capable of carrying 18,000+ containers, requiring extensive dock space and simultaneous use of multiple cranes.                                                                                                                                                             |
| **User Authentication**                 | relies on an external Identity and Access Management (IAM) provider (e.g., Google, Microsoft, Facebook), ensuring secure and centralized user verification.                                                                                                                 |
| **User authorization**                  | determining which features and data each user can access, is managed internally by the system according to their profile and assigned permissions, enforcing role-based access control across all modules.                                                                  |
| **Vessel**                              | is a ship or boat that carries cargo or passengers and arrives at a port to load or unload goods.                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Vessel Type**                         | classification of a ship based on its size and capacity, such as feeder, Panamax, Post-Panamax, or Ultra Large Container Vessel (ULCV), which affects its operational requirements at the port.                                                                             |
| **Vessel Visit**                        | represents the planned arrival and departure of a vessel at the port, including associated operations such as cargo loading and unloading.                                                                                                                                  |
| **Vessel Visit Notification**           | is a formal submission made by a shipping agent representative to inform the Port Authority about an upcoming vessel’s arrival and departure. It includes key details such as the vessel’s ETA, ETD, cargo information, and any special operational or safety requirements. |
| **Warehouses**                          | buildings within the port for storing cargo that requires additional handling, inspection, or special environmental conditions.                                                                                                                                             |
| **Yard Crane**                          | mobile cranes used within container yards to stack, move, and organize containers, supporting loading and unloading operations.                                                                                                                                             |
